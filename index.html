SCOTCH GAME


BASIC POSITION

Moves:

  1. e4 e5
  2. Nf3 Nc6
  3. d4

DESCRIPTION

The Scotch Game is a chess opening that begins with the moves 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.d4. It is known for its straightforward approach and is a good choice for players looking to avoid the complexities of other openings like the Ruy Lopez.



This opening is very popular to these days. However, beacause it is easy for black to make position equal, it is played less often in highest level.



First scotch game was documented in 18th century. It was correspondence match between London and Edinburgh in 1824.

This opening is used by white to try dominate center of the chessboard. Not only can white decide to exchange pawns on d4, but also they can play a gambit and develop their pieces faster then opponent.
